About

I am a dedicated and student-centered mathematics educator with several years of experience teaching at the high school level. I currently teach at Owatonna High School, and I have previously taught at Totino-Grace High School (I year), Bemidji High School (6 years), LTS Math teacher at Shakopee High School and Lincoln High School - TRF. I have experience teaching a wide range of learners, from students needing additional academic support to those enrolled in advanced courses such as Statistics and College Algebra.

My teaching philosophy centers on clarity, engagement, building mathematical confidence, and the belief that all students can succeed in mathematics. I am passionate about helping students not only understand mathematical concepts but also develop critical thinking skills that extend beyond the classroom. During my time at Bemidji, I developed and implemented the AP Computer Science Principles (CSP) curriculum, expanding the schools’ elective opportunities for students and fostering problem-solving and computational thinking skills.

I am committed to continuous professional growth and to contributing positively to the school communities I serve. My goal is to create inclusive, engaging learning environments where every student feels capable, supported, and challenged to succeed.
